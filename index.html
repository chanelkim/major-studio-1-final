About

Index of American Design

The Index of American Design comprises visual survey featuring over eighteen-thousand on paper documenting early American material culture. It showcases intricately rendered drawings that portrays decorative, folk, and popular art objects, spanning from the colonial period to the 20th century. Created between 1936 and 1942 within a division of the Federal Art Project—an initiative sponsored by the Works Progress Administration—the Index played a key role in providing work relief during the Great Depression for hundreds of artists and indexers across thirty-four states and the District of Columbia.

Project
& Partnership

More than a tenth of the National Gallery of Art’s collection comprises the Index of American Design. As an artifact, the Index symbolizes a historic era of federally supported arts programs and the aim to establish a national legacy. By cataloging the country’s design forms and craft objects, the Index sought to unite disparate regional styles under a shared and distinctly American identity. Despite its goal as archival reference, comprehensive access to the Index’s contents remain limited.

“Catalog of Connections'' provides a platform to celebrate and explore this extensive body in totality and still highlight the individual works for their astonishing detail. While the collection’s sheer number and fragile nature hinder physical exhibition and mass redistribution, digital interaction invites patrons to connect with the Index through levels of fidelity. By providing encapsulated views and frameworks for inquiry, this effort aligns with the NGA’s mission to deepen public understanding, curiosity and continuous learning.

Designer

Chanel Kim is a designer based in New York City, currently pursuing a Master of Science in Data Visualization at Parsons School of Design. As a digital product designer, she brings a diverse background to her work. Holding a Bachelor of Science in Human and Biological Anthropology, her professional journey includes creative campaigns, fine arts, small business administration, grassroots organizing, and hospitality.

Chanel is deeply curious about material culture and the concept of leisure, and is exploring these subjects at the intersection of data, design, technology, and social justice. Her diverse experiences contribute to a holistic understanding of the trans-disciplinary complexities within these fields, shaping her unique approach to design and innovation.
